The ageing consumer class

The world’s population is changing in ways that could barely be imagined a generation ago, and at a pace that is faster than any in recorded history. Not only are we all living longer, but in the richest countries – and in many newly middle class nations – people are having too few babies to keep population stable. By 2050, according to UN projections, those aged over 65 will outnumber children aged 5 and under, for the first time in human history.

While that may store up challenges many years ahead, it also means that adults around the world now have more money to spend and invest than ever. Old no longer means “poor”. Consumer spending by over-50s is expected to account for roughly 60 per cent of the US total and 50 per cent in the UK.

Industries, from cars to pharmaceuticals, and even the world of online dating, are having to adapt to this new generation of healthier, wealthier adults.
